Word Frequency

address

Word forms: addresses, addressing, addressedpronunciation note:   The noun is pronounced (ədrɛs ) or (ædrɛs ). The verb is pronounced (ədrɛs ).
1. countable noun
Your address is the number of the house or apartment and the name of the street and the town where you live or work.
The address is 2025 M Street, NW, Washington, DC, 20036.
2. countable noun
The address of a website is its location on the Internet, for example, http://www.thomson.com.
[computing]
Full details, including the website address to log on to, are at the bottom of this page.
3. transitive verb [usu passive]
If a letter, envelope, or parcel is addressed to you, your name and address have been written on it.
Applications should be addressed to: The business affairs editor.
4. transitive verb
If you address a group of people, you give a speech to them.
He is due to address a conference on human rights next week.
